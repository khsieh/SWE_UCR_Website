The Society of Women Engineers at UC Riverside annually hosts an entertaining hands-on space science community event for invited guests, Girl Scouts and students of the local community. Girl Scouts will be able to earn an event patch and work towards a badge. This event is typically free to all participants and will take place on the North side of the UC Riverside campus in the early Spring. We invite the faculty, staff, UCR, the Girl Scouts and our local community to take advantage of this opportunity. Elementary, Middle, and High School aged participants as well Community College and University students will enjoy a variety of events aimed at space science and engineering. Fun, hands-on activity centers geared toward children ages 5 and up will teach them about the the importance of the STEM fields in our everyday lives. They will have a chance to explore robotics, create their own unique star constellations in the planetarium, work on fun science and experiments and much much more. Every year we focus on different themes, which have included Biotechnology, Nanotechnology and Sustainability in the past. This event is made possible through co-sponsors including the Bourns Co., NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, the Riverside Astronomical Society, the Girl Scouts of San Gorgonio Council, UCR's Bourns College of Engineering, the ALPHA center at UCR, and the Society of Women Engineers.
