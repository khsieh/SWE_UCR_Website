Officer Elections

Being an Officer for SWE is a hard, but rewarding experience. Although it is time consuming, the leadership skills you gain, the friends you make, and the connections you keep are more than worth it! If you are interested in becoming an officer for SWE FY'13-'14, you've come to the right place.

President

  • Manage organization and other officers
  • Coordinate with SWE Nationals in filling out quarterly, financial and annual reports
  • Make sure SWE-UCR is in good standing with SWE Nationals
  • Organize officer and general meeetings
  • Make sure all other officers are on track with their individual projects
  • More Details
    •
    • Preside all meetings and come with an agenda of all events, updates, and responsibilities
      • Offical representative of SWE-UCR
        • SWE Headquarters, SWE Professional
        • Bourns College of Engineering, Leadership Council
        • Student Life Office, ASUCR with Treasurer
      • Check SWE Gmail regularly
      • Reports
        • Leadership Council Reports
          • Quarterly Reports
          • Continuous Feedback Forms (CFFs)
          • Dean's Funding Proposal (with Treasurer)
        • SWE
          • Annual Report
          • Annual Fiscal Report (with Treasurer)
          • Leadership Reporting: Change of officers contacts
          • Optional: Outstanding Collegiate Section (OCS) Nomination
    • In addition to your individual repsonsibilites, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Vice President
  • Assist President with her duties
  • Become acting President in case President cannot make it to an event
  • Usually attends LC (Leadership Council) Meetings
  • Acts as the enforcer (ensures that all officers are fulfilling responsibilities)
  • More Details
    •
    • Perform all duties in the absence of the President or at the request of the President
    • Attend Leadership Council and update President of the BCoE activities
    • Recruitment and Retention
      • This is important because a club is not sustainable without members.
      • As VP, working on advertising, brochures, posters, and word-of-mouth, are excellent recruitment methods. Long term benefits must also be visible so that retention can maintain.
    • In addition to your individual responsibilities, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Secretary
  • Takes notes at all meetings
  • Sends out Meeting updates and minutes to officers and members
  • Helps with President quarterly reports
  • More Details
    •
    • Meetings
      • Meeting Minutes
        • Start time, end time, attendance, and updates of the agenda (given by the President)
      • Take attendance: Pass a sign-in sheet around the room to gather names, majors, emails, & phone numbers
    • SWE Gmail
      • Check Daily
      • Add new contact information from sign-in sheets
      • Email everyone the meeting minutes by the end ofo the next day (This is to inform everyone who could not attend the meeting)
    • In addition to your individual responsibiities, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Treasurer
  • Fills out financial report at the end of the year
  • In charge of fundraisers (including tshirts)
  • Collects and deposits money into ASUCR account
  • Attends ASUCR Account Activation and Budget Hearing meeting
  • Is an authorized signature for reimbursement forms
  • More Details
    •
    • Liaison between ASUCR account/Dean's Funding and SWE
    • Keep detailed track of ASUCR account/Dean's Funding
    • Ensuring that enough money is allotted to different events whether it be social, professional, or outreach related
    • Working with the President when completing the Funding Proposal and Annual Financial Report
    • Reimburement
    • Fundraising Ideas
    • In addition to your individual responsibilites, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Outreach Coordinator
  • Organize and run SSED in the spring
  • Coordinate other outreach evetns at local high schools
  • Plans and runs activity for MESA Engineering Night
  • Maintains communication with local school and the ALPHA Center for more outreach opportunities
  • In charge of filling out and submitting outreach and program development grants
  • More Details
    •
    • Plan and execute Space, Science, & Engineering Day
    • Respond to Outreach Requests
      • Respond to emails and keep in contact with Outreach requests
      • Call for volunteers for outreach events
      • Organize carpool, if needed, for outreach events
    • Actively initiate and search for more ways to outreach to local school and community
    • Fills out and submit funding proposals. Including, but not limited to:
      • WOW Development Grant (SWE National)
      • Program Development Grant (SWE National)
      • ASUCR Outreach Grant (UC Riverside)
    • Record all outreach logs and statistics to SWE Nationals (SWEeter Futures Program)
    • In addition to your individual responsibilities, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Social Chair
  • Plan fun and effective social events throughout the year to get members feeling accepted into the SWE environment
  • Hosts SWE's Annual Ice Cream Social first week of school
  • Plans and executes SWE's Valentine Auction with Treasurers
  • Should encourage membership and member involvement by being present at tabling activities such as BearFacts, Dean's Orientation, Highlander and Discover Day
  • More Details
    •
    • Organizing and promoting social event, including but not limited to:
      • Annual Ice Cream Social
      • Holiday Potlucks
      • Valentine Auction (with Treasurer)
      • UCR Outdoor Excursion Activities
      • Officer Retreats
      • Arranging carpools when necessary
    • Actively initiate and plan new social events
    • In addition to your individual responsibilities, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Professional Development Chair
  • Plans events aimed towards professing members' professional development
  • Open and maintain communication with Career Center
  • Emails and invites speakers for meetings and/or panel events
  • More Details
    •
    • Liaison between the Career Center and SWE
      • Let SWE laways know what is available to them from the Career Center
      • Plan events with the cooperation of the Career Center, such as the Professional Dress Fashion Show
    • Plan and execute workshops/events that will help the members professionally
      • Alumni/Professional Panel
      • Mock Interviews/Resume workshops
      • Graduate school information
      • Invite industry speakers for meetings
    • In addition to your individual responsibilities, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Historian
  • Takes pictures and records our events
  • Hands pictures to webmaster
  • In charge of making new/maintaining posterboard (usually every year)
  • Should encourage membership and member involvement by being present at tabling activities such as BearFacts, Dean's Orientation, Highlander and Discover Day
  • More Details
    •
    • Event photographer
    • Work with webmaster in posting photos on the SWE website and facebook
    • Help make SWE posters/tri-folds
    • In addition to your individual responsibilites, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Webmaster
  • In charge of SWE website and Facebook account
  • Post up meeting minutes and pictures on SWE website
  • Maintains archive and the toaster
  • Keeps website and facebook updates with events and news
  • More Details
    •
    • Update SWE website
      • Promptly upload SWE minutes after Secretary sends them to you
      • Post new events/information as they are discussed in meetings
    • Update Facebook page as new events/information are/is discussed in meetings
    • Work with Historian to add photos to the website (using Picassa or Facebook)
    • Maintain and organize the toaster/server as needed
    • In addition to your individual responsibilites, each member should be willing to assist with other projects


    Applying for a Position


    *Being an officer for the Society of Women Engineers is a time consuming job. If you are elected into a position you are expected to attend officer and general meetings and maintain in constant communication with your fellow officers.

    To apply for an officer position, please fill out this application:
    Officer Application 2013 - PDF
    Officer Application 2013 - DOC

    And mail the application to swe@engr.ucr.edu with a picture to be used for the website.
